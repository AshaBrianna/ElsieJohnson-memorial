1930

Elsie was born Elsie Pauline Norman on April 8, 1930 in Kossuth, MS in Alcorn County. Her aunt, Cornealia "Neallie" Norman Orange, 29 at the time, served as the midwife, assisting Paul Norman, 27, and Corine Wims (sp?) Norman, 24, with the delivery.

1930s & 40s

The daughter of a sharecropper and a seamstress, Elsie grew up with two older brothers, Joseph L., and Edward L.. She attended the Mt. Pleasant Missionary Baptist, Macedonia Missionary Baptist, and Alcorn Baptist churches.

~ Map of Alcorn County from 1911 ~

late 1940s

Elsie graduated Corinth Colored High School with a 4.2 GPA, and spoke at graduation. She went on to attend Tougaloo College in Tougaloo, MS.

1950

Elsie was heavily involved in her college community. In addition to her studies, she was a member of the Paul Robeson Theatrical Society.

1950

In 1950, she crossed into the first Mississippi chapter of the Delta Sigma Theta Sorority. She would later travel with them on service trips as far as Japan.

~ Center in photo with sorority sisters ~

1950

While at Tougaloo, Elsie also met Ardee Johnson, a fellow Biology major and a member of the Football team and the Omega Psi Phi Fraternity. She married the McComb, MS native on September 7, 1950 in Pike County, MS.

~ Headshot from his Tougaloo senior page ~

1951

After being crowned “Ms. Tougaloo” in 1950, Elsie graduated on May 29, 1951 with a Bachelor's of Science in Biology. She was voted "Most Popular" in her senior class.

~ Center in crown during coronation ~

early 1950s

After graduation, Elsie and her husband moved to Washington, DC. While he attended dental school at Howard University, Elsie supported him by working for the NAACP. She soon gave birth to her first child, a daughter.

~ Original logo for the National Association for the Advancement of Colored People ~

late 1950s

When Air Force Captain Johnson began his service, Elsie joined him, traveling nationally and internationally to bases around the world and spending a substantial amount of time on a post-war tour in Japan. With Captain Johnson on tours, Elsie gave birth to her second child, a daughter, in Texas and her third child, a son, in Japan.

1960s

Following Captain Johnson's service, they settled down in Oakland, CA. Her husband opened a dental practice, and Elsie gave birth to her second son and fourth and final child.

1960s on

While Elsie dedicated herself to her family, she also remained active in the African-American community through work with the Delta Sigma Theta Sorority Inc., the Links Inc., and the Allen Temple Baptist Church. In her spare time, she loved bowling and music, especially gospel, Jazz, and R&B; and she was an avid dancer.

~ Seen with , a close friend from high school ~

1960s on

For the next 65 years, Elsie devoted herself to her family and to serving the Oakland community. She earned an Adult Education Full-Time Teaching Credential in 1977; she is remembered by many in the greater Bay Area for her years of work in the Oakland Public Schools system, and as an administrative assistant for California’s State Board of Equalization, the Department of Public Health, the Center for Infectious Diseases, and the Employment Development Department.

2020

At age 90, Elsie Pauline Norman Johnson passed away on Friday, September 4, 2020 in her home in Oakland, CA. Elsie is preceded in death by her brothers, Joseph L. & Edward L. She is survived by 2 daughters, Cheryl & Andrea, and 2 sons, Ardee & Christopher. Also joined in fond memories of Elsie are 9 grandchildren, 3 nieces, 1 nephew, and numerous friends.
