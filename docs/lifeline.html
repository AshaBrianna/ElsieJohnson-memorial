Elsie Pauline Norman Johnson

1930

On April 8, 1930, Elsie Pauline Norman Johnson was born in Kossuth, MS to Paul and Corine Norman. Her aunt, "Neallie" Norman Orange, served as the midwife.
~ Baby Elsie with mother, Corine ~

1930s & 40s

The youngest child of a sharecropper and a seamstress, Elsie grew up in Alcorn County with her two older brothers, Joseph L., and Edward L..
~ Map of Alcorn County from 1911 ~

1930s & 40s

From a young age, her parents emphasized to her the value of education and faith. She attended primary and secondary school in her hometown, and attended the Mt. Pleasant Missionary Baptist, Macedonia Missionary Baptist, and Alcorn Baptist churches throughout her adolescent years.
~ Center in white boots in school photo ~

1947

At 8 o'clock on Friday evening, May 30, 1947, Corinth Colored High School held their commencement ceremony. Elsie graduated with a 4.2 GPA, and delivered a speech, addressing her peers, friends, and family on the topic of "Vocation and Security".

late 1940s

She went on to pursue higher education and attend Tougaloo College in Tougaloo, MS.

1950

Elsie was heavily involved in her college community. In addition to her studies, she was a member of the Paul Robeson Theatrical Society.

1950

In 1950, she crossed into the first Mississippi chapter of the Delta Sigma Theta Sorority. She would later travel with them on service trips as far as Japan.

~ Center in photo with sorority sisters ~

1950

While at Tougaloo, Elsie also met Ardee Johnson, a fellow Biology major and a member of the Football team and the Omega Psi Phi Fraternity. She married the McComb, MS native on September 7, 1950 in Pike County, MS.

1951

Elsie was well regarded by her peers, and in 1950, she was crowned “Ms. Tougaloo”. She would later go on to also be voted "Most Popular" in her senior class.
~ Center in crown during Ms. Tougaloo coronation ~

1951

On May 29, 1951, Elsie graduated with a Bachelor's of Arts in Biology. Shortly after receiving her degree, she moved with her husband to Washington, D.C..
~ Center in crown during coronation ~

early 1950s

While Ardee Johnson pursued a Dental Degree from Howard University, Elsie supported him by working for the NAACP. She soon gave birth to her first child, a daughter.
~ Original logo for the National Association for the Advancement of Colored People ~

late 1950s

When Air Force Captain Johnson began his service, Elsie joined him, traveling nationally and internationally to bases around the world and spending a substantial amount of time on a post-war tour in Japan. With Captain Johnson on tours, Elsie gave birth to her second child, a daughter, in Texas and her third child, a son, in Japan.

1960s

Following Captain Johnson's service, they settled down in Oakland, CA. Her husband opened a dental practice, and Elsie gave birth to her second son and fourth and final child.

1960s on

While raising her four children, Elsie remained active in the African-American community through work with the Delta Sigma Theta Sorority Inc., the Links Inc., and the Allen Temple Baptist Church, where she served as a volunteer secretary, assisting Reverend Dr. J. Alfred Smith Sr..

1960s on

In her spare time, Elsie often went bowling with her friends, and even won a few trophies. She also loved music (especially gospel, Jazz, and R&B), and was an avid singer and dancer, often singing soprano solos in the choir at Allen Temple Baptist Church.
~ With high school friend, Walter F. ~

1960s on

For the next 65 years, Elsie continued to devote herself to her family and to serving the Oakland community. She earned an Adult Education Full-Time Teaching Credential in 1977; she is remembered by many in the greater Bay Area for her years of work in the Oakland Public Schools system, and as an administrative assistant for California’s State Board of Equalization, the Department of Public Health, the Center for Infectious Diseases, and the Employment Development Department.

2020

On Friday, September 4, 2020, the Lord called Elsie home. She passed away an her home in Oakland, CA. Elsie is preceded in death by her brothers, Joseph L. & Edward L. She is survived by 2 daughters, Cheryl & Andrea, and 2 sons, Ardee & Christopher. Also joined in fond memories of Elsie are 9 grandchildren, 3 nieces, 1 nephew, and numerous friends.
