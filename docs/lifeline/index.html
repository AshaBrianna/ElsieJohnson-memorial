Elsie Pauline Norman Johnson

APRIL 30, 1930 - SEPTEMBER 4, 2020

1930

Elsie was born to the late Paul and Corine Norman, on April 8, 1930 in Kossuth, MS.

1930s amp& 1940s

The daughter of a sharecropper and a seamstress, Elsie grew up in the church, attending Mt. Pleasant Missionary Baptist, Macedonia Missionary Baptist, and Alcorn Baptist churches.

late 1940s

Elsie graduated Corinth Colored High School with a 4.2 GPA, and went on to attend Tougaloo College.

1950

An active Tougaloo student, Elsie was a member of the Paul Robeson Theatrical Society, and crossed into the first Mississippi chapter of the Delta Sigma Theta Sorority in 1950. She was crowned “Ms. Tougaloo” that same year.

1950

While at Tougaloo, Elsie also met future husband, Ardee Johnson, a member of the Football team and Omega Psi Phi Fraternity. She married the McComb, MS native on September 7, 1950 in Pike County, MS.

1951

Elsie graduated from Tougaloo College on May 29, 1951 having earned a Bachelor's of Science in Biology. She was voted "Most Popular" in her senior class.

early 1950s

After graduation, Elsie and her husband moved to Washington, DC. While he attended dental school at Howard University, Elsie supported him while working for the NAACP (the National Association for the Advancement of Colored People).

late 1950s

When Air Force Captain Johnson began his service, Elsie joined him, traveling nationally and internationally to bases around the world and spending a substantial amount of time on a post-war tour in Japan.

1964

Elsie gave birth to her seconnd son, and fourth and final child inn 1964.

1960s

Following Captain Johnson's service, they settled down in Oakland, CA, where her husband openned a dental practice.

1951

For the next 65 years, Elsie dedicated herself to her family and to serving the Oakland community. She is remembered by many in the greater Bay Area for her years of work in the Oakland Public Schools system, and as an administrative assistant for California’s State Board of Equalization, the Department of Public Health, the Center for Infectious Diseases, and the Employment Development Department. Throughout her life, Elsie also remained active in the African-American community through work with the Delta Sigma Theta Sorority Inc., the Links Inc., and the Allen Temple Baptist Church. In her spare time, she loved bowling and music, especially gospel, Jazz, and Ramp&B; and she was an avid dancer.

2020

At age 90, Elsie Pauline Norman Johnson passed away on Friday, September 4, 2020 in her home in Oakland, CA. Elsie is preceded in death by her brothers, Joseph L., and Edward L. She is survived by her 4 children: daughters, Cheryl (Paul) Nath and Andrea (Dwayne) Wilkes; and sons, Ardee and Christopher. Also joined in fond memories of Elsie are 9 grandchildren, 3 nieces, 1 nephew, and numerous friends.
